Featured Article

Comparing Energy Efficiency Strategies: Lessons from Sweden for Nigeria

Sweden shows a very compelling case of how high energy consumption does not have to translate into high emissions...

By contrast, Nigeria’s energy system is limited by inefficiencies, reliance on fossil fuel, and underutilization of renewables.

“98.5% of Sweden’s electricity came from renewable or nuclear sources” (Swedish Institute, 2024).

Nigeria relies heavily on gas turbines and generators, which are costly and polluting.

Three priorities stand out:

  • Scale decentralized solar mini-grids
  • Introduce renewable policy incentives
  • Invest in energy efficiency technologies
